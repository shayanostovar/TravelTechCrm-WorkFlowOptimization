# TravelTech CRM Workflow Optimization

## Overview

This repository documents the design, implementation, operational use, and evolution of a CRM-driven customer acquisition and lead management framework within a travel agency environment between approximately 2017 and 2020.

The project was not limited to CRM software implementation. It involved designing operational workflows, lead qualification processes, customer follow-up procedures, reporting frameworks, customer satisfaction mechanisms, and management visibility systems across multiple business units.

The initiative was implemented at ParastooSeir Travel Agency, a travel services organization providing airline ticketing, tour packages, visa services, and other travel-related solutions.

The primary objective was to create a structured customer lifecycle management process capable of connecting marketing activities, inbound customer inquiries, sales operations, customer follow-up, and management reporting.

---

# Business Context

As customer acquisition channels expanded through digital marketing, search engines, landing pages, social media campaigns, SMS marketing, and email marketing, the organization required a centralized system capable of tracking customer interactions and measuring operational performance.

Customer inquiries were arriving from multiple channels, but there was limited visibility into:

* How many inquiries became leads
* How many leads received follow-up
* Which marketing channels generated qualified customers
* Which destinations were most requested
* How customer behavior varied across different service categories

Without a structured CRM process, operational decisions relied heavily on assumptions and manual reporting.

To address these challenges, a CRM initiative was launched and Microsoft Dynamics CRM was selected as the platform.

---

# Key Business Objectives

The CRM initiative focused on several strategic objectives:

* Standardize lead registration
* Improve customer visibility
* Create measurable sales funnels
* Improve follow-up consistency
* Enable customer segmentation

# Major Components

## CRM Strategy and Platform Selection

Evaluation and selection of Microsoft Dynamics CRM as the primary customer management platform.

## Lead Qualification Framework

Service-specific qualification processes for:

* Airline ticket inquiries
* Tour package inquiries
* Visa service inquiries

## Issabel PBX Integration

Automatic caller identification and lead creation support through integration with the organization's telephone system.

## Sales Funnel Visibility

Tracking customer progression from marketing acquisition to final conversion.

## Follow-Up and Happy Call Process

Structured post-inquiry engagement and customer follow-up activities.

## KPI and Reporting Framework

Measurement of lead generation, customer conversion, marketing effectiveness, destination demand, and operational performance.

## Customer Satisfaction and Complaint Management

Formalized customer satisfaction measurement and complaint handling processes supported by documented operational procedures.

## CRM Migration Strategy

Analysis of limitations within Microsoft Dynamics CRM and business drivers that eventually led to migration toward VTiger CRM.

---

# Key Lessons

Several important lessons emerged from the project:

* CRM success depends more on process design than software selection.
* Lead qualification quality directly impacts reporting quality.
* Customer visibility requires integration across multiple systems.
* Reporting should be designed around business decisions, not only operational metrics.
* Customer satisfaction processes must be embedded into operational workflows.
* CRM platforms provide limited value when disconnected from sales and financial systems.
